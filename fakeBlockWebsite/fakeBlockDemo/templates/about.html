Inspiration

Ever since the term ‘fake news’ started floating around in 2016 United States election, I knew it was going to be a big problem going forward. With the 2020 election coming up soon (at the time of making this project), I decided to make an effort do something to combat this monster of a problem at one of its biggest sources, Facebook, by creating the FakeBlock Chrome browser extension. With this project, my goal was to create a tool, that was as politically unbiased as possible, to attempt to spot fake news by only the contents of a Facebook post (Truly, an impossible task. Try identifying fake news from only a title sometime). But, I decided to proceed with my flawed idea in order to get people to notice that while on Facebook, they are constantly absorbing news: and often from a variety of sources that may have less integrity than an organization with the time, money and willingness to fully investigate a story before passing it on. And believe me, FakeBlock will find those stories, and it may or may not flag them because it has a difficult job.

For a single college student, this has been an enormous task and a rewarding learning experience. I hope that using (or even just seeing) FakeBlock helps you think more critically about where you get your news from and to remain critical of sensationalist news stories. While I am aware that my own efforts to tackle this problem have fallen far short of the "perfect tool" mark, I hope that it may at least spark conversation and thought on the complex topics of fake news and fact checking.
